About TOEIC Listening & Reading Test

The TOEIC Listening and Reading Test is a two-hour multiple-choice test that consists of 200 questions divided into two sections. It is designed to assess how well you can communicate in English in business, commerce, and industry settings.

Listening Section

  • 100 questions (45 minutes)
  • Four parts: Photographs, Question-Response, Conversations, Talks
  • Score range: 5 to 495 points

Reading Section

  • 100 questions (75 minutes)
  • Three parts: Incomplete Sentences, Text Completion, Reading Comprehension
  • Score range: 5 to 495 points

TOEIC Score Interpretation

The TOEIC Listening and Reading Test provides scores on a scale from 10 to 990 points. Your score reflects your English proficiency level in workplace contexts.

Score Range Proficiency Level Description
905-990 Advanced Can communicate effectively in any business situation
785-900 Upper Intermediate Can communicate effectively in most business situations
605-780 Intermediate Can communicate in many business situations

TOEIC Listening Section Overview

The Listening section consists of 100 questions administered by CD player. It assesses your ability to understand spoken English in various contexts. You'll have 45 minutes to complete this section.

1 Part 1: Photographs (6 questions)

You will look at a photograph and listen to four statements. You must select the statement that best describes the photograph.

Sample Question:

0:45

2 Part 2: Question-Response (25 questions)

You will hear a question or statement followed by three responses. You must select the most appropriate response to the question or statement.

Sample Question:

0:12

3 Part 3: Conversations (39 questions)

You will listen to a conversation between two or more people. Then you will answer 3-4 questions about the content of the conversation.

4 Part 4: Talks (30 questions)

You will listen to a short talk (announcement, advertisement, etc.). Then you will answer 3 questions about the content of the talk.

Listening Strategies

Before Listening

  • Read directions and questions carefully
  • Predict what you might hear
  • Be aware of the question types

During Listening

  • Focus on key information (who, what, where, when, why)
  • Pay attention to tone and attitude
  • Listen for signal words and transitions

Time Management

  • Move on if you miss a question
  • Use the time between questions to prepare for the next one
  • Answer all questions, even if you have to guess

Common Pitfalls

  • Getting distracted by unfamiliar words
  • Trying to understand every single word
  • Translating to your native language

TOEIC Reading Section Overview

The Reading section consists of 100 questions to be completed in 75 minutes. It assesses your ability to understand written English in workplace contexts. This section is divided into three parts.

5 Part 5: Incomplete Sentences (30 questions)

You will read a sentence with a missing word or phrase. You must select the correct answer from four choices to complete the sentence.

Sample Question:

The marketing department is ________ a new advertising campaign for the summer product line.

6 Part 6: Text Completion (16 questions)

You will read a text with four missing words or phrases. You must select the correct answer from four choices for each gap.

Sample Question:

To: All Staff
From: Human Resources
Subject: Office Relocation

We are pleased to announce that our company will be moving to a new office location next month. The new building is [1] in the downtown business district and offers more space and modern facilities. The relocation is [2] for the weekend of July 15-16 to minimize disruption to our operations.

[1]

7 Part 7: Reading Comprehension (54 questions)

You will read various texts (letters, emails, articles, etc.) and answer questions about the content. Some texts may be paired with related information.

Sample Text Types:

  • Emails and memos
  • Articles and reports
  • Advertisements and notices
  • Forms and tables
  • Letters and messages

Reading Strategies

Skimming & Scanning

  • Skim for general meaning before reading in detail
  • Scan for specific information like dates, names, or numbers
  • Read questions first to know what to look for

Text Structure

  • Identify the text type (email, article, etc.)
  • Pay attention to headings, subheadings, and formatting
  • Look for topic sentences at the beginning of paragraphs

Time Management

  • Allocate time based on question difficulty and point value
  • Don't spend too much time on any single question
  • Leave difficult questions and return if time permits

Common Pitfalls

  • Getting stuck on unknown vocabulary
  • Reading too slowly and running out of time
  • Misunderstanding the question's intent

Study Tips

Consistent Practice

Regular, shorter study sessions are more effective than cramming. Aim for daily practice, even if it's just 30 minutes.

Focus on Weaknesses

Identify your weakest areas through practice tests and dedicate more time to improving them.

Review Mistakes

Don't just move on after a practice test. Carefully review each mistake to understand why you got it wrong.

Manage Test Anxiety

Practice under test-like conditions to build familiarity and reduce anxiety on test day.

FAQ

The TOEIC test is typically offered monthly in most test centers, but frequency may vary by location. Check the official ETS website for specific dates in your area.
TOEIC scores are valid for two years from the test date. After that, you may need to retake the test if a current score is required.
A "good" score depends on your goals. Generally, 700+ is considered strong for most business positions, 850+ is excellent, and 900+ is exceptional. Check with your employer or institution for specific requirements.
